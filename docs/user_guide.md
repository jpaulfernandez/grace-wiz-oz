# Grace Prototype — User Guide

## GRACE-W-03: Women Participant View (Phone)

You are going to interact with **Grace** as a woman participant on a mobile phone. The session takes about 15–20 minutes. A side panel on the left will guide you step by step — tap what it points to on the phone, then tap **Next step** to move forward.

You will work through five short scenarios:

1. **A workplace situation.** You open the Companion — Grace's chat space — and talk through an uncomfortable situation with a coworker. You'll use a suggested prompt, send it, and when the Companion replies, you'll choose to let the conversation go without saving it.
2. **Heading to work.** You open the Companion again to talk about work anxiety. This time, you'll carry the conversation into your journal, write a sentence about how you're feeling, and save the entry. After saving, Grace offers a short breathing pause.
3. **Looking back over time.** Grace looks across your past journal entries and highlights patterns — repeated themes or phrases. You'll tap a highlighted phrase to read what Grace noticed, then close it.
4. **Writing with a prompt.** You open the Reflective Journal, choose Guided mode, and save an entry with pre-filled prompts — no writing required unless you want to.
5. **Writing it down as a record.** You open the Incident Log from the bottom navigation. The form is pre-filled with details you can review. After saving, Grace shows a timestamp receipt, and you choose "Do nothing for now" to wrap up.

At the end, you'll answer four short reflection questions before finishing.

## GRACE-L-01: Lawyer / Advocate View (Desktop)

You are going to interact with the **lawyer/provider dashboard** on a desktop screen. A prospective client has submitted a consultation request through Grace, and you'll walk through the intake process from the receiving provider's perspective.

1. **Dashboard.** A notification appears for a new consultation request from Jane. You'll open it.
2. **Client profile.** You'll review a redacted client profile — identifying details are held back until you formally accept. Tap **Accept Request** to proceed and unlock evidence access.
3. **Request records.** The client has shared encrypted incident logs and journal entries. You'll request access to both — access is gated and logged on both sides.
4. **Corpus chat.** You'll query the client's shared entries using three suggested questions: timeline, evidence, and whether the client has named the conduct in her own words. Each answer cites the specific entry and paragraph it draws from.
5. **Intake notes.** Review the AI synthesis and add your own intake note. Your notes are kept separate from the client's entries.
6. **Certified export.** Review the export preview — a timestamped, hash-chained document you could attach to a pleading. Tap **End Review** to finish.

At the end, you'll answer three short reflection questions about the legal synthesis, hash-receipt model, and whether you'd accept shared links from clients.

## GRACE-C-01: Clinician View (Desktop)

You are going to interact with the **clinician/provider dashboard** on a desktop screen. A prospective patient has shared her Grace artifacts and requested an initial consultation. You'll walk through the intake from the receiving clinician's perspective.

1. **Dashboard.** A notification appears for a new referral from Jane. You'll open it.
2. **Patient profile.** You'll review a redacted patient profile — full details are held back until you accept. Tap **Accept Request** to unlock access.
3. **Request artifacts.** The patient has shared encrypted incident logs and journal entries. You'll request access to both — access is gated and logged on both sides.
4. **Somatic chat.** You'll query the patient's shared somatic logs using three suggested questions: somatic patterns, panic reactions, and attachment style indicators. Each answer cites the specific entry it draws from.
5. **Session-prep notes.** Review the clinical synthesis and add your own session-prep note. Your notes are kept separate from the patient's entries.
6. **Export.** Review the export preview — a timestamped summary you could file in the patient's record. Tap **End Review** to finish.

At the end, you'll answer three short reflection questions about the clinical synthesis, whether you'd recommend a patient use this between sessions, and whether you'd accept shared links from patients.
